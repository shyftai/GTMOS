## Example 1: SaaS → VP Sales (Cold Outbound)

We helped [similar company] cut ramp time from 90 days to 40 by turning their best calls into a repeatable playbook.

Worth a look?

**Touch 2 — Re: your sales team (42 words):**
Separate thought — saw [industry report] came out this week showing 67% of mid-market sales teams lose their first deal to "no decision" not competitors.

The teams that fix this usually nail discovery. Happy to share what we're seeing.

**Touch 3 — Re: your sales team (38 words):**
No ask on this one — just thought this might be useful.

[Case study company] published their onboarding framework that cut ramp time by 55%. Relevant to where {{company}} is right now.

**Touch 4 — Re: your sales team (24 words):**

## Example 2: E-commerce tool → Head of Growth (Signal-Triggered)

**Context:** Selling a conversion optimization tool. Signal: company just raised Series A.

**Subject:** re: the raise

**Touch 1 (52 words):**
Saw {{company}} closed a Series A — congrats.

Most DTC brands post-raise pour budget into acquisition but conversion rate stays flat. The math breaks when CAC rises faster than AOV.

[Customer] fixed this after their Series A — 34% lift in conversion, paid for itself in 6 weeks.

Make sense to chat?

**Touch 2 — Re: the raise (36 words):**
Quick follow-up — didn't want this to get buried.

One thing I keep seeing post-raise: brands scale ad spend 3x but don't touch their landing pages. The gap compounds fast.

Open to comparing notes?

## What makes these work

| Principle | How it shows up |
|-----------|----------------|
| Peer voice | Reads like a colleague, not a marketer |
| Observation-led | Every touch 1 opens with something about THEM |
| Ruthless brevity | Touch 1: 52-68 words. Follow-ups: 22-44 words |
| Interest-based CTA | "Worth a look?" / "Curious?" / "Make sense to chat?" |
| Angle rotation | Every touch uses a different angle |
| Subject lines | 2-4 words, lowercase, no punctuation |
| No spam words | Zero "excited", "thrilled", "game-changing" |
| Specificity | Concrete numbers, named companies, real signals |
