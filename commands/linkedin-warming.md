# Command: LinkedIn Content Warming

## Trigger
"Warm up [list/segment] on LinkedIn" or "Run LinkedIn warming before outreach"

## What it is
A pre-outreach engagement strategy on LinkedIn. Before sending a connection request, build familiarity by engaging with the prospect's content. This increases connection acceptance rates and reply rates.

## Steps
1. Load the target list segment (contacts about to enter a LinkedIn sequence)
2. For each contact, check their LinkedIn activity via Crispy:
   - Have they posted in the last 30 days?
   - Have they commented on or liked relevant content?
   - Are they active enough for warming to matter?

### Warming sequence (3-7 days before connection request)
| Day | Action | Notes |
|-----|--------|-------|
| Day 1 | View their profile | Shows up in "Who viewed your profile" |
| Day 2-3 | Like one of their recent posts | Choose something relevant, not random |
| Day 4-5 | Leave a thoughtful comment on a post | 1-2 sentences, add genuine value — no sales pitch |
| Day 6-7 | Send connection request with personalized note | Reference something specific (their post, a shared interest, a signal) |

### Rules
- Only warm contacts who are active on LinkedIn (posted in last 30 days)
- For inactive profiles: skip warming, go straight to connection request
- Comments must be genuine — add a perspective, ask a question, share a relevant experience
- Never comment with "Great post!" or generic praise — that's transparent
- Do not mention your product or company in warming comments
- Space actions across days — do not like + comment + connect all at once
- Track warming status per contact in the campaign list

### Skip warming if:
- Contact hasn't posted in 60+ days
- You have a mutual connection who can intro
- A time-sensitive signal makes speed more important than warming
- Contact is already a 1st-degree connection

3. After warming period, move to standard connection request via Crispy
4. Connection note should feel natural — reference the engagement if appropriate
5. Log warming actions in campaign logs/decisions.md
