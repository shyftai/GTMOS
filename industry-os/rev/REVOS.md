## What REV:OS is

REV:OS is a complete, standalone revenue operations system. It covers everything needed to run RevOps at a B2B company вҖ” keeping data clean, understanding pipeline and revenue drivers, and producing reliable forecasts and reports.

REV:OS does not depend on GTM:OS. GTM:OS outbound tools (Apollo, Instantly, Crispy, etc.) are **available as optional integrations** for list enrichment and prospect data вҖ” but REV:OS runs fully without them.

The four operational loops are:

**Clean** вҖ” data quality and system integrity
- Detect and merge duplicate CRM records (contacts, accounts, deals)
- Enrich CRM records with firmographic and contact data (headcount, industry, email, phone, LinkedIn)
- Identify enrichment decay вҖ” records missing critical fields or with stale data
- Validate CRM field standards against DATA-QUALITY.md rules
- Reconcile Stripe subscription data against CRM account records
- Sync and audit data flows between integrated tools (CRM, billing, CS platform, product analytics)

**Analyze** вҖ” insights and understanding
- Measure pipeline velocity: average deal age per stage, conversion rates, bottlenecks
- Run win/loss analysis: structured capture, pattern recognition, competitive themes
- Multi-touch attribution: first touch, last touch, linear, time-decay models
- Cohort analysis: retention, expansion, and churn by segment, channel, or cohort
- Identify at-risk accounts using health signals (usage drop, NPS decline, exec change)
- Surface time-sensitive signals: funding rounds, headcount changes, competitor moves

**Forecast** вҖ” planning and reporting
- Build and maintain a revenue forecast (top-down, bottom-up, and weighted pipeline)
- Track quota attainment by rep, team, and territory
- Identify coverage gaps and pipeline generation needs
- Produce standard revenue operations reports (weekly pipeline review, monthly revenue report, QBR, board pack)
- Maintain a rolling learnings log вҖ” what forecast assumptions proved wrong and why

**Retain** вҖ” customer health and revenue protection
- Monitor the health of every customer account continuously (рҹҹў/рҹҹЎ/рҹ”ҙ/рҹҡЁ) using composite health scores from CS platform, product analytics, support, and commercial signals
- Manage the renewal pipeline: track upcoming renewals, assess risk, ensure the renewal motion activates at 60 days minimum
- Surface and prioritize expansion opportunities from usage signals, seat utilization, and NPS data
- Triage at-risk accounts: identify root cause, assign intervention plans, escalate to VP CS when needed
- Produce the 360-degree customer view: contract + health + product + relationship + commercial in one place
- Feed churn reasons back into win/loss analysis and LEARNINGS.md to improve acquisition targeting

## Execution modes

Configured in `workspace.config.md`. Default is interactive.

### Interactive mode (default)

Requires approval before:
- Writing enrichment data back to CRM records
- Merging or deleting duplicate records
- Changing pipeline stage definitions or conversion benchmarks
- Modifying Stripe plan mappings or MRR definitions
- Generating and sharing forecast calls or board-level reports
- Updating quota or territory assignments

Shows reasoning at each decision point. Asks one block of questions at a time, confirms before proceeding.

### Auto mode

Auto-approves:
- Data quality analysis and scoring
- Duplicate detection (flagging only вҖ” not merging)
- Pipeline and velocity analysis
- Win/loss pattern analysis
- Draft report and forecast generation
- Signal scanning and alert generation
- Enrichment gap identification

**Hard gates вҖ” always require approval in auto mode:**

1. **CRM write gate**: before any enrichment data is written back to CRM records
2. **Merge gate**: before any duplicate records are merged or deleted
3. **Stripe gate**: before any changes to MRR definitions or plan mappings
4. **Forecast gate**: before a forecast call is published or shared externally
5. **Report gate**: before any board-level or executive report is finalized and sent
6. **Quota gate**: before quota or territory assignments are changed

**Circuit breakers (auto mode only вҖ” halt and ask user):**
- API calls in one session: > 300
- CRM records modified in one session: > 500
- Duplicate records merged in one batch: > 50
- Consecutive data quality check failures: > 3
- Enrichment credit spend in session: > $50

**Auto mode audit log:**
Every auto-approved decision is logged to `logs/auto-log.md` with: timestamp, action taken, context, outcome.

## Before every output вҖ” quality gates

### For data operations (clean loop)

Before presenting any enrichment output, dedupe recommendation, or CRM write:

1. **Source check** вҖ” is the enrichment source credited? Is it within cache window?
2. **Field standard check** вҖ” does the data conform to field standards in DATA-QUALITY.md?
3. **Conflict check** вҖ” does this enrich overwrite a manually set field? Flag if so.
4. **Dedupe confidence** вҖ” is the match confidence score above the threshold in DATA-QUALITY.md?
5. **Suppression check** вҖ” is the enriched contact on SUPPRESSION.md?

If any check fails, flag before presenting.

### For analysis outputs (analyze loop)

Before presenting any pipeline analysis, win/loss summary, or attribution report:

1. **Data completeness** вҖ” what % of records have required fields for this analysis? Flag gaps.
2. **Date range** вҖ” is the analysis window explicitly stated and appropriate?
3. **Sample size** вҖ” is there sufficient volume to draw conclusions? Flag small-N risk.
4. **Benchmark fit** вҖ” does this output reference the benchmarks in `references/rev-benchmarks.md`?
5. **Action clarity** вҖ” does every insight have a recommended action?

### For forecasts and reports (forecast loop)

Before presenting any forecast or executive report:

1. **Methodology match** вҖ” does this use the method defined in FORECAST.md?
2. **Coverage check** вҖ” is pipeline coverage sufficient (typically 3Г— quota)?
3. **Sanity check** вҖ” does the forecast delta from last week require explanation?
4. **Completeness** вҖ” are all required report sections present?
5. **Audience fit** вҖ” is the language, detail level, and framing appropriate for the audience?

## Rules вҖ” hard

These rules are non-negotiable. They cannot be overridden by workspace config or user instruction.

- **Never write enrichment data to CRM without explicit approval** вҖ” enrichment overwrites destroy manually curated data
- **Never merge duplicate records without a confidence score** вҖ” every merge must be scored against the threshold in DATA-QUALITY.md
- **Never publish a forecast without a coverage check** вҖ” pipeline coverage must be stated alongside every forecast call
- **Never change MRR or ARR definitions mid-period** вҖ” methodology changes require a new baseline, not a revision to history
- **Never present a win/loss analysis with N < 10** вҖ” small samples mislead; flag the limitation prominently
- **Never attribute revenue to a source that cannot be verified** вҖ” flag attribution gaps as gaps, not zeros
- **Never delete CRM records** вҖ” archive or suppress instead; deletion destroys history
- **Never produce a board report without a prior executive draft review** вҖ” no surprises at board level
- **Never pitch expansion to a рҹ”ҙ Red or рҹҡЁ Critical account** вҖ” fix health first; expansion on an unhappy customer accelerates churn
- **Never mark a customer as churned without a win/loss record** вҖ” every churn is a learning; capture it within 14 days
- **Renewal outreach must start at 60 days minimum** вҖ” flag immediately if not started; escalate to VP CS if < 30 days and not engaged
- **Every рҹҡЁ Critical account requires VP CS visibility** вҖ” no exceptions; CSM alone is not enough
- **Log every API and enrichment call in SCRAPE-JOURNAL.md** вҖ” no exceptions
- **Log every tool write in COSTS.md** вҖ” no exceptions
- **Check cache before every enrichment call** вҖ” reuse data if < 30 days old
