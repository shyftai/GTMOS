# Ecommerce Promotional Calendar

Key dates, promotional spacing rules, and BFCM planning framework.

---

## Promotional spacing rules

- **Minimum 7 days between major promotions** — audience fatigue and brand dilution
- **BFCM blackout: 48 hours before BFCM launch** — no new campaign launches
- **Post-BFCM: 2-week pause** — before holiday campaigns resume
- **Product launches: 2-week minimum runup** — required for sufficient audience warming
- **January low-promotion month** — customers are spent out after Q4, promotional intensity should be low

### By brand type
| Brand positioning | Max promotions/year | Notes |
|-------------------|---------------------|-------|
| Premium / Luxury | 2–3 | BFCM optional, brand anniversary, exclusive drops only |
| Mid-market | 6–8 | Seasonal + holiday + brand moments |
| Value / Deal-driven | 10–12 | Ongoing acceptable, but never consecutive weeks |

## BFCM planning framework

**8 weeks out:** Strategic decisions
- Confirm offer structure (discount depth, bundles, threshold gifts)
- Inventory check — ensure sufficient stock for all BFCM-featured SKUs
- Tech check — confirm platform can handle 10x traffic

**6 weeks out:** Creative and copy
- Brief all creative assets (email templates, ad creatives, banner designs)
- Write all email copy and subject line variants
- Build ad campaigns in draft (allows early disapproval resolution)

**4 weeks out:** Build
- Email and SMS flows: set up BFCM sequences in ESP
- Ad campaigns: fully built, in review, ready to go live
- Landing pages: live and tested
- Suppression lists: refreshed

**2 weeks out:** Test and activate
- Test all email links, UTMs, and tracking
- Test checkout flow with discount codes
- Set up abandoned cart for BFCM variant (different urgency copy)
- Launch soft announcement to VIP subscribers

**Launch week:** Execute
- BF: All channels live by 8am brand time zone
- Monitor hourly: ROAS, CVR, stock levels
- Mid-day email if strong performance ("selling fast")
- SMS for high-urgency segments

**Post-BFCM:**
- 2-week low-promotional period (subscriber fatigue is high)
- Shift to gift-giving and last-order-date messaging
- Begin win-back for BFCM browsers who didn't buy
