# Ecommerce Benchmarks

Industry benchmarks by metric category and revenue stage. Use to assess performance and set realistic targets.

---

## Site performance benchmarks

| Metric | Below average | Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|--------------|---------|------|-----------|
| CVR (overall) | < 1% | 1–2% | 2–3.5% | > 3.5% |
| Cart abandonment rate | > 85% | 70–80% | 60–70% | < 60% |
| Add to cart rate | < 5% | 5–10% | 10–15% | > 15% |
| Page speed LCP (mobile) | > 4s | 2.5–4s | < 2.5s | < 1.5s |
| Bounce rate | > 65% | 50–65% | 40–50% | < 40% |
| Pages per session | < 1.5 | 1.5–2.5 | 2.5–3.5 | > 3.5 |

**Notes:**
- CVR varies significantly by category (fashion 1–2%, supplements 2–4%, electronics 0.5–1%)
- AOV is category-dependent — do not benchmark across categories

## Financial benchmarks by revenue stage

| Stage | Gross margin | Contribution margin | Marketing % of revenue | MoM growth |
|-------|-------------|---------------------|----------------------|-----------|
| $0–$1M/yr | 45–60% | 25–40% | 25–40% | 15–25% |
| $1–$5M/yr | 50–65% | 30–45% | 20–35% | 10–20% |
| $5–$20M/yr | 55–65% | 35–50% | 15–25% | 8–15% |
| $20M+/yr | 55–70% | 40–55% | 10–20% | 5–12% |

**Notes:**
- Gross margin varies widely by category (supplements 65–75%, apparel 50–60%, furniture 40–50%)
- Contribution margin is the more important metric — it reflects true marketing profitability
- Marketing % of revenue naturally compresses as retention and organic grow

## How to use these benchmarks

1. Compare current metrics (METRICS.md) against benchmarks in the relevant category row
2. Flag any metric in "Below average" — prioritize improving before scaling
3. "Good" is the target for a healthy, scaling brand
4. "Excellent" is worth celebrating but check for statistical noise before optimizing away from what's working
5. Do not over-weight a single metric — look at clusters (e.g. low CVR + low AOV = price or fit problem, not a CRO problem)
