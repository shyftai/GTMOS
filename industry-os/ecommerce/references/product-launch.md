# Product Launch Framework

A complete playbook for launching a new product or restocking a sold-out hero SKU.

---

## Launch types

| Type | Description | Timeline | Key difference |
|------|-------------|----------|----------------|
| Full launch | Brand-new product, new audience | 4-week runup | Maximum buildup, widest activation |
| Soft launch | New variant or minor addition | 2-week runup | Existing audience only, no paid push |
| Restock launch | Sold-out hero SKU returning | 1-week runup | Waitlist-first, urgency angle |
| Exclusive drop | Limited inventory, scarcity-driven | 2-week runup | VIP first, then general |

---

## 4-week launch timeline (full launch)

### Week 4 — Content creation

- [ ] Product photography — hero, lifestyle, detail shots, UGC seeding
- [ ] Video content — demo or tutorial (30–60s for ads, 3–5min for email/YouTube)
- [ ] Email copy — all 5 launch emails written and reviewed
- [ ] Ad copy — 3–5 headline variants, 3 primary text variants
- [ ] SMS copy — launch announcement + early access messages
- [ ] Landing page / PDP — built, copywritten, images loaded
- [ ] Submit ad creative to Meta/Google for early review (allow 5–7 days for disapproval risk)

### Week 3 — Audience building

- [ ] Waitlist email capture — dedicated opt-in form or pop-up for this product
- [ ] Social teaser posts — 2–3 posts hinting at the launch without revealing
- [ ] Influencer briefing — send brief + product to creators for seeding
- [ ] VIP segment email — "Something big is coming" preview to top customers
- [ ] Set up back-in-stock / notify-me on PDP if applicable

### Week 2 — Build out and pre-activate

- [ ] All email flows for this product configured (post-purchase with product-specific cross-sell)
- [ ] Email sequences loaded in ESP — ready to send, not yet scheduled
- [ ] Paid ad campaigns fully built — targeting set, creative approved, UTMs verified
- [ ] Checkout and cart set up for this SKU
- [ ] Test full purchase flow end to end (add to cart → checkout → confirmation email)
- [ ] Review page / Okendo / Yotpo setup for new SKU
- [ ] Influencer posts going live this week — generate early social proof

### Week 1 — Final checks and VIP early access

- [ ] All assets and copy reviewed — no placeholder text, all links live
- [ ] Suppression list refreshed
- [ ] Calendar check — no conflicting promo in CALENDAR.md
- [ ] Inventory check — PRODUCTS.md shows sufficient stock for projected demand
- [ ] UTM check — all campaign and email links have UTMs
- [ ] VIP early access email sent (24–48 hours before general launch)
- [ ] SMS early access to SMS subscribers (if applicable)

## Post-launch cadence (first 7 days)

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 2 | Email to non-openers with new subject line |
| Day 2 | Review initial data: ROAS, CVR, add-to-cart rate |
| Day 3 | If strong sales: social proof email — "X people have already tried it" |
| Day 5 | Mid-week check: inventory velocity vs. forecast |
| Day 7 | Week 1 results email: performance summary + social proof |
| Day 7 | Review ROAS and pause any underperforming ad sets |

---

## Launch KPIs

Track these within first 72 hours:

| KPI | Measure | Flag if... |
|-----|---------|-----------|
| Day 1 revenue | vs. forecast | < 50% of forecast on Day 1 |
| Email CVR | vs. list average | < 50% of average list CVR |
| Paid ROAS | vs. existing product ROAS | < 1.0x on Day 1–3 |
| Add-to-cart rate | vs. site average | < 5% (PDP issue) |
| Return rate (Day 14) | vs. category average | > 15% early (product quality signal) |

---

## Inventory planning for launches

- **Conservative:** Stock for 2x expected Day 1 sales
- **Recommended:** Stock for 4x expected Day 1 sales
- **Flag:** If inventory projects to run out within 7 days of launch, pause paid scale
- **OOS rule (hard gate):** If stock drops below 10 units, pause all paid ads featuring this SKU immediately

### Pre-launch inventory threshold check

Calculate: Expected daily velocity × 14 days (minimum) = minimum stock needed for launch.
If PRODUCTS.md shows less: delay launch or reduce paid spend budget.

---

## Common launch failure modes

| Failure | Root cause | Prevention |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| OOS within 48 hours | Inventory underplanned | Stock minimum 4x day-1 forecast |
| Ad account disapprovals | Submitted too late, policy issues | Submit ads 7 days early |
| No email momentum | No waitlist built | Run 2-week teaser capture campaign |
| Low Day 1 sales | Price too high, no social proof | Seed influencers 2 weeks before for UGC |
| High return rate | Product doesn't match marketing claims | Align copy with actual product experience |
| Email fatigue | Launch emails annoyed existing customers | Segment — suppress recent buyers from teaser sequence |

## Restock launch (1-week timeline)

When a sold-out SKU is coming back:

**Day 7 before restock:**
- Email to waitlist: "It's back in [X] days — you're first to know"
- SMS to waitlist: Short announcement with early-access link

**Day 3 before restock:**
- Email to full list: "Our bestseller is almost back — get on the early access list"

**Restock day:**
- Email to waitlist: Exclusive early access (12-hour window before general)
- SMS to waitlist: Early access announcement
- Paid ads: Go live with "Back in stock" messaging
- Email to full list (6 hours after waitlist): General announcement
- Social post

**Key message:** Use scarcity honestly. "We only restocked [X] units" if true. Do not manufacture urgency.
