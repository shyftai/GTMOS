# Client Reporting Templates

Client-facing report formats. Load before generating any client report or QBR deck.

---

## Reporting principles

- **Report on outcomes, not activity.** Clients don't care how many posts were published — they care what changed in their business.
- **Be honest about underperformance.** A report that only shows wins loses credibility. Show targets, show actuals, explain gaps.
- **Match the metric to the brief.** Only report the metrics agreed in the original brief. Adding unrelevant metrics that look good is spin.
- **Short is professional.** A 3-page monthly report is better than a 15-page one. Cut everything that doesn't change a decision.
- **Send before the call, not during.** Reports are pre-read material. The call is for decisions, not data walkthrough.

## Weekly update (async — Slack or email)

**Format:** Short text block or bullet list. Not a document.
**When:** Every Monday morning (or agreed day)
**Sent by:** Account lead or channel specialist

**Template:**

```
[Client Name] — Week of [Date]

SPEND THIS WEEK: $[amount]

KEY METRICS:
- [Metric 1]: [value vs. target]
- [Metric 2]: [value vs. target]
- [Metric 3]: [value vs. target]

TOP 3 THIS WEEK:
1. [Activity completed]
2. [Activity completed]
3. [Activity completed]

BLOCKERS / REQUESTS:
- [Anything needed from client — access, approvals, decisions]

NEXT WEEK:
- [Priority 1]
- [Priority 2]
- [Priority 3]
```

**Length:** Max 200 words. If it's longer, cut it.
**Do not include:** Long narrative, background context, praise for the agency.

## Monthly report

**Format:** Document or slide deck (max 8 pages / 8 slides)
**When:** Within 5 business days of month end
**Sent by:** Account lead
**Context:** Send with a 3-sentence email summary — client should understand the result before opening the report

**Template structure:**

### Page 1: Executive summary

TL;DR for the CMO or Founder who won't read the rest.

```
Month: [Month Year]

Overall status: On track / Behind / Ahead
One-line summary: [E.g. "Strong paid search month — CPL down 18%. Organic slower than planned."]

Key wins:
- [Win 1 with number]
- [Win 2 with number]

Key issues:
- [Issue 1 — honest description]
- [What we're doing about it]

Next month focus:
- [Priority 1]
- [Priority 2]
```

### Page 2: Results vs. targets

Table format. Required.

| Metric | Target | Actual | vs. Target | vs. Last Month |
|--------|--------|--------|------------|----------------|
| [Agreed metric 1] | X | Y | +/- Z% | +/- Z% |
| [Agreed metric 2] | X | Y | +/- Z% | +/- Z% |

Only include metrics agreed in the original brief. Do not add vanity metrics.

### Pages 3–5: Channel breakdown

One page per active channel.

**Paid Search:**
- Spend: $[amount]
- Impressions / Clicks / CTR
- Conversions / CPL / CPA
- Top campaigns: [name, spend, result]
- Changes made this month
- What's working / what isn't

**SEO & Content:**
- Organic sessions vs. last month
- New rankings moved (track top 20 keywords)
- Content published
- Backlinks acquired
- Technical issues resolved

**Email & Lifecycle:**
- Emails sent / open rate / click rate / unsubscribe rate
- Flows triggered / conversions generated
- Revenue attributed (if e-commerce)
- List growth

**Paid Social:**
- Spend / impressions / reach
- Clicks / CTR
- CPL or ROAS
- Creative performance: top 3 ads

### Page 6: Top wins

Maximum 3. Specific and quantified.

"Rebuilt ad creative for [campaign] — CTR improved from 0.4% to 1.2%."
"Fixed site tracking gap — recovered attribution on 23% of previously untracked conversions."

### Page 7: Learnings and optimisations

What the data told us this month and what we're changing.

| Observation | Action taken | Expected impact |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| | | |

### Page 8: Next month focus

3 priorities + agreed targets.

| Priority | Owner | Metric | Target |
|----------|-------|--------|--------|
| | | | |

**Budget reconciliation (appendix if needed):**

| Channel | Budget | Spent | Remaining | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|-----------|-------|
| | | | | |

## QBR deck

See `retainer-workflows.md` for the full 12-slide QBR structure and agenda.

Quick reference:

1. Cover
2. Executive summary (3-bullet TL;DR)
3. Results vs. targets (table)
4–6. Channel breakdowns
7. Top wins
8. What didn't work (honest)
9. Learnings + what changes
10. Next quarter plan (3 priorities)
11. Next quarter targets
12. Questions / open items

**Deck rules:**
- Max 12 slides — if you need more, you have too much information
- Send 3 days before the call
- The call is for discussion, not for reading slides aloud
- Tone: honest, confident, not defensive

---

## Report delivery checklist

Before sending any report:

- [ ] All metrics match what was agreed in the original brief
- [ ] All numbers are accurate (double-check against tool dashboards)
- [ ] Underperforming areas are acknowledged and explained
- [ ] No claims without supporting data
- [ ] No agency-branded self-congratulation
- [ ] Budget reconciliation is accurate
- [ ] Next steps are clear and owned
- [ ] Length is appropriate — cut anything that doesn't change a decision

## Anomaly alerts

When a metric moves significantly (>20% off target in either direction), alert the client proactively — don't wait for the monthly report.

**Format (Slack or email):**

```
[Client Name] — [Metric] Alert

[Metric] is [X% above/below] target this week.

What happened: [1–2 sentences — honest explanation]
What we're doing: [1–2 sentences — action taken or being taken]
Expected outcome: [When should this resolve?]

No action needed from you — just keeping you in the loop.
```

**Alert thresholds:**
- Paid search ROAS / CPL: >20% off target
- Email open rate: drops >15% week-over-week
- Organic traffic: drops >20% week-over-week (could signal technical issue)
- Ad spend: overpaces or underpaces by >15%
- Spam rate: any increase above 0.1%

Proactive alerts prevent the "why didn't you tell me sooner" conversation.
