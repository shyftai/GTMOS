# Capacity Planning

Team utilization targets, capacity allocation rules, warning signs, and hiring triggers. Load before running `/agency:capacity` or evaluating whether to take on a new client.

---

## Utilization targets

| Utilization | Status | Action |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| < 50% | Underutilized | Revenue or new business problem — escalate immediately |
| 50–60% | Below target | Review pipeline and new business urgency |
| 60–75% | Healthy | Optimal zone — productive without burnout risk |
| 75–80% | High but manageable | Monitor weekly; do not add new client commitments |
| 80–90% | At risk | Stop taking new work until capacity frees up or new hire starts |
| > 90% | Critical | Quality will suffer; delivery SLAs will slip; team will burn out |

Track utilization weekly. Review at the start of every session via `/agency:capacity`. Update TEAM.md after every review.

---

## Capacity allocation rules

These hours are always reserved — they are not available for client work:

1. **New client onboarding:** reserve 5 hrs/week per Account Manager for the first 4 weeks of a new client
2. **QBR prep:** block 2 hrs per retainer client per quarter (schedule 2 weeks in advance)
3. **New business support:** 4–6 hrs/week for BD lead during active pitch or proposal phases
4. **Internal ops and admin:** 10–15% of total capacity always held back (approx. 4–6 hrs/week per FTE)
5. **Team learning and development:** 1–2 hrs/week per person (if below 75% utilization)

The remaining capacity after these reserves is what is available for client deliverables.

## Capacity by service line (guideline for 1 FTE at healthy utilization)

| Role | Active client load |
|------|-------------------|
| Account Manager | 6–8 clients at standard retainer scope |
| Paid Search Specialist | 4–6 clients (active weekly optimization); 8–10 (reporting and light management only) |
| SEO / Content Specialist | 4–6 clients at 4 articles/month each |
| Email Specialist | 8–12 clients at 2 sends/month each |
| Designer | Depends heavily on asset volume — track by hours, not client count |
| Copywriter | 5–7 clients with ongoing retainer copy needs |
| Strategist | 4–6 clients at senior strategy support level |
| Analyst | 8–12 clients for reporting only; 4–6 if building dashboards |

These are guidelines, not rules. Actual capacity depends on scope complexity, client communication intensity, and revision volume.

## Early warning signs

These patterns precede a capacity crisis. Act on them before the crisis, not during:

- **Any team member > 80% for 2+ consecutive weeks:** hire or reduce scope — do not wait
- **Single client consuming > 40% of one person's time:** bus factor risk; redistribute or add resource
- **Deliverable SLA misses two weeks in a row:** capacity or process issue — investigate immediately
- **Team member reporting stress or overtime consistently:** leading indicator of imminent departure
- **New business pipeline closing faster than team can onboard:** a good problem that still requires a plan

## When to hire

Hiring is warranted when:

1. Agency utilization consistently > 80% for 3 months
2. MRR > $50K with fewer than 3 FTE (leverage is too low; margin is compressed)
3. Deals are being lost because of stated capacity constraints (track Closed Lost reason in PIPELINE.md)
4. QBR NPS declining due to responsiveness or delivery speed issues
5. A key team member is at > 85% utilization for 2+ months (departure risk)

### Hiring order of operations (typical small agency)

1. First hire: Account Manager — frees up the founder or principal to close new business
2. Second hire: Specialist in the highest-volume service line
3. Third hire: Second Account Manager or senior Strategist
4. Fourth hire: Dedicated Analyst / Operations (at ~$1M ARR)

### Freelancer vs. full-time

- Use freelancers for: surge capacity, specialist skills on one-off projects, overflow on a specific service line
- Hire full-time for: ongoing retainer capacity, account management, client-facing roles
- Freelancer cost: typically 30–50% higher per hour than equivalent FTE — budget accordingly in FINANCE.md

## Capacity planning before onboarding a new client

Before committing to a new client, run this checklist:

- [ ] Does the team have > 20% headroom at current utilization?
- [ ] Is there an Account Manager with capacity for a new client?
- [ ] Is there specialist capacity in the service lines this client needs?
- [ ] Is the onboarding buffer (5 hrs/week for 4 weeks) reserved?
- [ ] Are there any existing clients in a critical delivery phase that could be disrupted?

If any answer is No: discuss with the team before signing. Do not promise a start date until capacity is confirmed.
