# Retainer Workflows

QBR, renewal, and upsell workflows for existing clients. Load before running `/agency:retainer-renewal`, `/agency:qbr-prep`, or `/agency:upsell`.

---

## Renewal workflow

Starts 60 days before contract end. Non-negotiable timeline — starting later creates pressure that leads to bad decisions.

### Day -60: Internal health review

Before any client conversation, the account lead reviews internally:

**Questions to answer:**
- What is the client's current health status? (CLIENTS.md)
- What results have been delivered vs. targets set?
- What's changed on their side since the contract started? (personnel, product, market)
- What's changed on our side? (team, capability, pricing)
- What should the renewal look like? Same scope / upsell / downscale?
- Are there any relationship risks to address before the renewal conversation?

**Output:** A one-paragraph internal note on each client up for renewal. This informs the QBR and renewal proposal.

**If health = Red:** Do NOT begin renewal conversation yet. Run escalation protocol first (see below).

---

### Day -45: QBR

Run the quarterly business review before the renewal conversation. The QBR frames the renewal.

**Rule:** Never start a renewal negotiation without first running a QBR. The QBR surfaces results, alignment, and any issues — without it, the renewal conversation happens in an information vacuum.

See full QBR structure below.

---

### Day -30: Renewal proposal

After QBR, send the renewal proposal. Three options to present:

**Option A: Renew at same scope**
"Results are on track. We'd like to continue as is. Here's what Q[next] looks like."

**Option B: Renew with expansion (upsell)**
"Results are strong. We think [new service] is the next growth lever. Here's what adding it would look like."

**Option C: Adjust scope down (save the relationship)**
If results are mixed or client is tight on budget — offer to reduce scope rather than lose the client entirely. "We can reduce to [service subset] at [lower price] to maintain continuity while [situation changes]."

**Proposal format:** 1-page. Not a pitch — a renewal summary.
- Results delivered vs. targets
- What continues
- What changes (if anything)
- New terms and price
- Next step: sign by [date]

---

### Day -14: Follow-up if no signed renewal

One email follow-up. Not a chase — a practical note.

"[Name] — following up on the renewal for [month]. We need to confirm the team allocation for [next month] this week. Happy to jump on a call if anything's changed."

---

### Day -7: Final outreach

"Confirming next steps for [company]'s engagement. If we don't hear back by [date], I'll plan to hold the team capacity and reach out for next steps."

This creates a practical deadline without being aggressive.

---

### Day 0: No response protocol

If contract expires and no renewal is signed:
1. Send a formal hold notice: "We've paused active work on [company] pending renewal confirmation."
2. Do not ghost — do not just stop working without communication
3. Do not continue working without a signed agreement
4. Log in PIPELINE.md as "Renewal — pending"

## Escalation protocol (Red health clients)

When a client is marked Red before renewal:

**Step 1: Internal debrief (week 1)**
- Account lead + agency leadership
- Root cause: is it results, communication, team, expectations, or external?
- Decide: can this be fixed? Is it worth fixing?

**Step 2: Escalation call (week 2)**
- Account lead + senior sponsor (director or partner)
- On the client side: ask for a call with the most senior decision maker
- Agenda: "We want to share our assessment and hear your honest feedback"
- Do not run this over email

**Step 3: Recovery plan or offboarding**
- If fixable: document a 30-day recovery plan with clear milestones
- If not fixable: begin a professional offboarding process
- Never let a Red client silently churn — it damages referral potential and leaves things unresolved

## QBR structure

**Frequency:** Every 90 days for all retainer clients, without exception.
**Duration:** 45 minutes
**Format:** Video call — not async
**Deck:** Maximum 12 slides, sent to client 3 days before the call
**Owner:** Account lead presents; agency leadership attends for key clients

### QBR agenda

**Minutes 0–10: Results recap**
- Metrics vs. targets set at the last QBR
- Highlight: what exceeded expectations?
- Acknowledge: what underperformed and why?

Format as a simple table:

| Metric | Target | Actual | Status |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| [Metric 1] | X | Y | On track / Behind / Exceeded |

**Minutes 10–20: What worked / what didn't**
- Maximum 3 "worked" points — be specific about why
- Maximum 3 "didn't work" points — be honest about the cause, not defensive
- What does this tell us about the next quarter?

**Minutes 20–35: Next quarter plan**
- 3 strategic priorities
- Specific campaigns or initiatives
- Any scope changes?
- Targets for next QBR

**Minutes 35–45: Questions and alignment**
- Open to client questions
- Check: does the client feel heard?
- Is there anything else they'd like us to address?

**After the call (within 24 hours):**
- Send a written summary: decisions made, next steps, owners, dates
- Update CLIENTS.md with QBR date and health status
- Set next QBR reminder (90 days)
- If renewal is upcoming: trigger renewal workflow

---

### QBR deck structure (12 slides max)

1. Cover: client name, period, agency name
2. Executive summary: 3-bullet TL;DR
3. Results vs. targets: table or chart
4. Channel 1 breakdown: what we ran, what happened
5. Channel 2 breakdown
6. Channel 3 breakdown (if applicable)
7. Top wins this quarter
8. What didn't work and why (honest)
9. Learnings and what we're changing
10. Next quarter plan: 3 priorities
11. Next quarter targets: agreed metrics
12. Questions / open items

**Tone:** Honest, confident, not defensive. If results are below target, say so directly with a clear explanation and a recovery plan. Clients trust agencies that acknowledge failure more than agencies that spin it.

## Upsell trigger points and approach

### When to surface an upsell

| Trigger | Timing | Upsell angle |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| Client hitting goals for 2+ consecutive months | Month 2–3 of strong results | "What's the next growth lever?" |
| Client hiring for a role the agency covers | When job posting appears | "We can do that — and we're already up to speed on your business" |
| Client expanding to a new market | When announced | "New market needs a different playbook — we've done this" |
| Client launching a new product | 4 weeks before launch | "Launch → need distribution. We should run this campaign." |
| Client running SEO → pitch email lifecycle | At QBR | "Organic traffic is up. Are you converting it? Let's add lifecycle email." |
| Client running paid → pitch attribution | At month 2 | "You're spending well. Let's make sure you can prove what's working." |
| Client running 2 channels → pitch full-service | At 6-month mark | "You're getting results across two channels. The next step is pulling them together under one strategy." |

### Upsell approach

**Rule:** Never pitch an upsell before results are established. A client who hasn't seen results yet will interpret an upsell as the agency trying to make more money, not help them.

**Timing:** First upsell conversation at earliest 60 days into the engagement, ideally at QBR.

**Format:** 1-page upsell brief (not a full proposal):
- What they're currently getting
- The gap or opportunity
- What the upsell service would do
- One proof point from a comparable client
- The investment (one tier recommendation)
- The next step

**Approach in conversation:**
Frame as a natural extension, not a sales pitch. "Based on what we're seeing in your data, the next thing to unlock is [X]. Here's why, and here's what it would look like."

## Client health monitoring

**Frequency:** Review health status monthly at minimum. Update CLIENTS.md immediately if health changes.

**Health change triggers:**

Green → Yellow:
- Results miss target two months in a row
- Client stops attending scheduled calls
- Slow email responses from primary contact
- Scope creep or unclear expectations emerging

Yellow → Red:
- Client escalates a complaint
- Results significantly miss target (>30% below)
- Client requests to pause or reduce scope
- Primary contact changes (new budget holder = relationship reset)

Red → Recovery or Offboarding:
- 30-day recovery plan executed with measurable milestones
- If milestones not met: begin professional offboarding process

**Health update command:** Update CLIENTS.md health column after any status change. Claude will surface Red clients in `/agency:portfolio`.
