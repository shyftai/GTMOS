# Agency Buyer Personas

Deep profiles for the three primary buyers of agency services. Load the relevant persona before writing any copy or preparing any pitch.

---

## Persona 1: CMO (Chief Marketing Officer)

### Profile

**Seniority:** C-suite
**Company size:** 100–500 employees (smaller companies often have VP Marketing instead)
**Reports to:** CEO or COO
**Owns:** Marketing budget, brand, pipeline contribution, agency relationships
**Day-to-day:** Board prep, team leadership, agency management, budget justification

### Goals

- Drive brand growth and category awareness
- Contribute meaningfully to pipeline (board measures this in pipeline coverage and CAC)
- Hit quarterly OKRs — often tied to comp
- Build and develop a capable marketing team
- Demonstrate the ROI of marketing to a sceptical CEO or CFO

### Fears

- Wasted agency budget with no measurable impact (they own this risk)
- Missing pipeline targets in a quarter — career damage
- Overcommitting to an agency that underdelivers and making them look bad
- Slow agencies that require constant hand-holding and management overhead
- Agencies that use vanity metrics to hide underperformance

### How they think about agencies

The CMO has almost certainly worked with multiple agencies before. They have opinions. They are not impressed by pitch decks. They respond to:
- Accountability: "Here's exactly how we'd measure success with you"
- Proof: "Here's what we did for a company in your position — exact numbers"
- Speed: "We can have a plan in front of you in 7 days"
- Peer respect: Talk to them as an equal, not as a vendor

They are wary of agencies that:
- Can't explain their methodology
- Hide behind busy-ness and process
- Fail to report on the metrics the board actually cares about

### Buying process

- Strategic decision maker — they sign off on contracts
- For >$15K/month: they decide; for smaller engagements they may delegate to VP
- Will involve their VP Marketing in evaluation
- References and case studies matter — will do due diligence
- Sales cycle: 21–45 days for a motivated CMO
- Best introduced: warm referral or specific signal-triggered outreach

### Agency relationship dynamics

- Sets direction, owns the budget, measures outcomes
- Unlikely to be in weekly status calls — that's the VP's job
- Wants monthly/quarterly reporting with clear attribution to business outcomes
- Will renew if the agency makes them look good to the board

### Copy guidance

- **Max length:** 80 words first touch / 50 words follow-up
- **CTA:** "Worth 20 minutes?" — strategy call framing, not "demo"
- **Open with:** One specific result from a comparable company, or a specific observation about their business
- **Never:** Tactical detail in first touch, content calendar mentions, jargon, compliments
- **Best angle:** "We report on the same metrics your board sees"
- **Best send time:** Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9am or 5–7pm (before/after work hours)
- **Reads on:** Mobile, scanning — subject line and first line decide everything

### Objections

- "Happy with current agency" → challenge on unserved channels or unproven attribution
- "Too expensive" → ROI reframe: output vs. cost, not price comparison
- "We do it in-house" → position as extending the team on one channel

## Persona 2: VP Marketing / Head of Marketing / Marketing Director

### Profile

**Seniority:** Senior Manager to VP / Director
**Company size:** 50–500 employees — the most common buyer at this range
**Reports to:** CMO (if exists) or directly to CEO
**Owns:** Campaign execution, team output, MQL targets, vendor management
**Day-to-day:** Running campaigns, managing the team, reporting on results, managing agencies

### Goals

- Hit quarterly MQL and pipeline targets (their performance review depends on this)
- Manage team capacity — always stretched, never enough hours in the day
- Prove the ROI of every dollar to justify the budget at next planning cycle
- Look competent and reliable to the CMO or CEO above them
- Get campaigns out the door on time

### Fears

- Running out of team capacity and missing targets because of it
- Budget cuts if results don't hold
- Picking an agency publicly that fails — they recommended it
- Agencies that are great in the sales process but slow after signing
- Wasted onboarding time with no results in the first 60 days

### How they think about agencies

The VP Marketing is both the recommender and the day-to-day operator. They are evaluating:
- "Can I trust them to deliver without me babysitting them?"
- "Will they make me look good to my CMO?"
- "Do they actually understand my space, or will I spend 3 months explaining context?"

They are more likely to respond to cold outreach than a CMO because cold outreach from agencies is legitimately relevant to them — they're always looking for execution help.

### Buying process

- Day-to-day decision maker for agencies under $10K/month
- For larger engagements: recommends to CMO, often champions internally
- Does their homework — will check your case studies, website, LinkedIn, Clutch
- Will want to speak to a reference before signing
- Sales cycle: 14–30 days for a motivated VP

### Agency relationship dynamics

- Primary day-to-day contact once agency is onboarded
- Cares about execution quality, communication speed, and accuracy of reporting
- Wants weekly async updates and ability to get quick answers
- Most likely to churn an agency if results don't show in first 90 days
- Most likely to renew if the agency makes their quarterly reviews easier

### Copy guidance

- **Max length:** 100 words first touch / 60 words follow-up
- **CTA:** "Worth a 30-minute working call?" — frame it as practical, not strategic
- **Open with:** Specific result in their vertical, or a pain point observation about their situation
- **Never:** Generic "we do everything" pitch, claiming to be "full-service" without specifics, vague claims without data
- **Best angle:** Execution bandwidth — "you have enough on your plate, here's what we'd take off it"
- **Best send time:** Tuesday–Thursday, 10am–12pm or 2–4pm (active working hours)
- **Reads on:** Laptop during work hours — more likely to read fully than CMO

### Objections

- "We already have an agency" → challenge on gaps, not on replacing the whole relationship
- "Not the right time" → plant the seed for a follow-up in 60 days
- "Our team can handle it" → agree, position for overflow, specialist channels, or surge capacity

## Persona 3: Founder / CEO (acting as CMO)

### Profile

**Seniority:** Founder, CEO, Co-Founder, MD, Owner
**Company size:** 20–150 employees — typically no dedicated CMO yet
**Reports to:** Board (if funded) or nobody (if bootstrapped)
**Owns:** Everything — including marketing decisions by default
**Day-to-day:** Sales, product, hiring, investor relations, and yes, somehow marketing too

### Goals

- Growth — at speed, without burning cash
- Simple, accountable relationships — they don't want to manage the agency
- Outcomes: leads, pipeline, revenue — not activity metrics or vanity stats
- Agency should feel like an internal team extension, not a vendor relationship

### Fears

- Slow agencies that deliver polished decks and delays instead of results
- Hidden fees and scope creep (they've been burned)
- Losing control of brand voice or messaging
- Committing to a long contract for something that might not work
- Agencies that need too much hand-holding

### How they think about agencies

Founders are the most direct buyers. They decide fast when they see value. They walk away fast when they sense spin. They're reading your email at 10pm on their phone while trying to decompress from a brutal day.

What gets them: one clear result, fast ask, no fluff. What loses them: long email, setup/context, jargon, anything that sounds like a 12-slide deck is coming.

They are the most likely to buy from a referral — they trust peer recommendations over any marketing.

### Buying process

- Final budget authority — no internal approvals needed
- Can decide in 1–2 conversations if they trust you
- May want a pilot or discovery sprint before a full retainer
- Least likely to ask for formal proposals — more likely to say "just tell me what you'd do"
- Sales cycle: 7–21 days if there's conviction; or stuck for months if uncertainty

### Agency relationship dynamics

- Wants to be informed but not involved in the day-to-day
- Measures agency on one thing: is the business growing?
- Will become a strong advocate (and referrer) if they trust the agency
- May churn if they don't see results within 60 days or feel out of the loop

### Copy guidance

- **Max length:** 60 words maximum — if it needs more, cut it, don't justify it
- **CTA:** "15 minutes?" — make it as easy as possible
- **Open with:** The result, not the setup. Bottom line first.
- **Never:** Long proposals, jargon ("holistic", "leverage", "synergy"), anything that sounds expensive without justifying it, three-paragraph setups before the actual point
- **Best angle:** Speed, directness, accountability — "we don't need hand-holding, and we don't give you any"
- **Best send time:** Monday–Wednesday early morning (7–8am) or evening (8–9pm)
- **Reads on:** Mobile, evenings and weekends

### Objections

- "Too expensive" → relate to the cost of not growing, not to competitor pricing
- "Tried an agency before and it didn't work" → validate the frustration, then differentiate specifically
- "Not the right time" → offer a small pilot or 30-day sprint to reduce the commitment

## Persona comparison

| Factor | CMO | VP Marketing | Founder |
|--------|-----|-------------|---------|
| Decision authority | Full (>$15K/mo) | Often (< $10K/mo) | Full always |
| Email length limit | 80 words | 100 words | 60 words |
| CTA | 20-min strategy call | 30-min working call | 15-min intro |
| Primary fear | Wasted budget / looking bad to board | Missing targets / team capacity | Speed / control |
| Best signal | Results + accountability | Results + bandwidth | Results + directness |
| Cold outreach conversion | Lower (more selective) | Medium (most responsive) | Higher (if message fits) |
| Reads on | Mobile, scanning | Laptop, during work | Mobile, evening |
| Referral weight | High | Medium | Very high |
