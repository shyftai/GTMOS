# Personas — [Agency Name] New Business

Three primary buyer personas for agency new business. Load the relevant persona before writing any copy.

---

## Persona 1: CMO (Chief Marketing Officer)

**Job titles:** CMO, Chief Marketing Officer, SVP Marketing
**Seniority:** C-suite
**Company size:** Typically 100–500 employees (smaller companies have VP instead)
**Reports to:** CEO or COO

### Goals
- Brand growth and market positioning
- Pipeline contribution and revenue attribution (CAC, pipeline coverage, LTV)
- Board-level marketing metrics — they present to board quarterly
- Build and develop a high-performing marketing team

### Fears
- Wasted budget with no proof of impact
- Missing pipeline targets — career risk
- Looking bad to the CEO or board
- Slow agencies that require constant hand-holding
- Overpromising agencies who underdeliver

### How they buy
- Strategic owner of agency relationships, but often delegates day-to-day to VP/Director
- Will become aware through LinkedIn, peer referrals, or being contacted directly
- Skeptical of cold outreach — needs proof, not promises
- Responds best to: specific result + specific method + clear accountability
- Decision maker for contracts over $15K/month

### Agency relationship dynamics
- Sets the strategic direction, owns the budget
- Measures agency on business outcomes, not activity metrics
- Wants monthly/quarterly reporting with clear attribution
- Will renew if agency makes them look good to the board

### Copy rules for CMO
- **Max length:** 80 words for first touch, 50 words for follow-ups
- **CTA:** 20-minute strategy call (not a demo)
- **Lead with:** One specific result from a comparable company
- **Avoid:** Tactical detail in first touch, anything that sounds like "content calendar", any use of "synergy", "holistic", "leverage"
- **Best angle:** ROI and accountability — "we report the same metrics your board sees"
- **Reads on:** Mobile, scans rather than reads
- **Best send time:** Tuesday–Thursday 7–9am or 5–7pm

### Objections (see agency-objections.md for full playbook)
- "We're happy with our current agency" — challenge on unserved channels
- "We do it in-house" — position as extending the team, not replacing it
- "Too expensive" — ROI reframe, not price justification

## Persona 2: VP Marketing / Head of Marketing / Marketing Director

**Job titles:** VP Marketing, VP of Marketing, Head of Marketing, Marketing Director, Director of Marketing
**Seniority:** Director to VP
**Company size:** 50–500 employees — most common buyer at this size

### Goals
- Hit pipeline and MQL targets (their performance review depends on this)
- Manage team capacity — they have too much to do with too few people
- Prove ROI on every dollar to justify budget renewals
- Look good to the CMO or CEO above them

### Fears
- Running out of capacity — team is stretched
- Budget cuts if results don't show
- Picking an agency that fails publicly
- Missing quarterly pipeline targets

### How they buy
- Day-to-day decision maker for agencies under $10K/month
- Recommends vendors to CMO for larger engagements
- Evaluates on: proof of results, communication style, fit with their team
- More likely to respond to cold outreach than CMO if it's relevant and specific
- Does more research — will check your case studies, LinkedIn, website

### Agency relationship dynamics
- Primary day-to-day contact once agency is hired
- Cares about execution quality as much as strategy
- Wants weekly updates, clear ownership, fast communication
- Most likely to churn agency if results don't show within 60–90 days

### Copy rules for VP Marketing
- **Max length:** 100 words for first touch, 60 for follow-ups
- **CTA:** 30-minute working call (show you'll come prepared)
- **Lead with:** Specific result in their vertical, or a pain point observation
- **Avoid:** Generic "we do everything" pitch, buzzwords, vague claims
- **Best angle:** Execution bandwidth + specific results in their industry
- **Reads on:** Laptop, during work hours (10am–3pm local)
- **Best send time:** Tuesday–Thursday 10am–12pm or 2–4pm

### Objections
- "We already have an agency" — challenge on gaps, not on replacing
- "Not the right time" — plant seed for 60-day revisit
- "Our team can handle it" — agree, position for overflow or specialist work

## Persona 3: Founder / CEO (acting as CMO)

**Job titles:** CEO, Co-Founder, Founder, MD (Managing Director), Owner
**Seniority:** C-suite operator
**Company size:** 20–150 employees — typically no dedicated CMO yet

### Goals
- Growth at speed without burning cash
- Simple, accountable reporting — they don't want to manage the agency
- Agency should feel like an internal team member, not a vendor
- Clear business outcomes: leads, pipeline, revenue

### Fears
- Slow agencies that deliver polished decks instead of results
- Hidden fees and scope creep
- Losing control of brand direction
- Committing to long contracts for something that might not work

### How they buy
- Final budget authority — no other approvers
- Buys on trust and directness more than credentials
- Will read referrals from peers — warm intro wins here
- Short attention span for cold outreach — 3 seconds to hook or they're gone
- Often reads email on phone, evenings or weekends

### Agency relationship dynamics
- Wants to be in the loop but not in the weeds
- Measures success in revenue, not vanity metrics
- Will advocate hard internally if they like the agency
- Most likely to refer the agency to their network

### Copy rules for Founder
- **Max length:** 60 words maximum — if it's longer, cut it
- **CTA:** 15-minute intro call
- **Lead with:** Bottom line first — the result, not the methodology
- **Avoid:** Long proposals, jargon, anything that sounds expensive, buzzwords
- **Best angle:** Speed, directness, accountability, no hand-holding needed
- **Reads on:** Mobile, evenings and weekends
- **Best send time:** Monday–Wednesday 7–8am or 8–9pm (commute / evening check)

### Objections
- "Too expensive" — relate to cost of not growing
- "We tried an agency before, it didn't work" — validate the experience, differentiate
- "Not the right time" — offer a small pilot or discovery sprint to reduce risk
