# Tone of Voice — [Agency Name]

## Core voice: peer, not pitch

Write as a peer — a marketing professional sharing something genuinely useful, not a vendor selling services. The person reading this has been pitched by 20 agencies this month. Sound different by not sounding like a pitch.

---

## Voice principles

**Peer, not salesy**
Write like you're emailing a colleague. Short sentences. Direct observations. No wind-up.

Good: "Saw you're hiring a demand gen manager. Usually means the team is stretched on outbound."
Bad: "We're excited to reach out and share how our cutting-edge demand generation solutions could transform your marketing function."

**Confident, not arrogant**
State what you know. Don't hedge. Don't oversell.

Good: "We've done this for three SaaS companies at your stage. Here's what worked."
Bad: "We believe we may be uniquely positioned to potentially help companies like yours achieve their growth objectives."

**Evidence-led, not claim-led**
Proof wins. Specifics win. Vague claims get ignored.

Good: "Took [Company] from 2% reply rate to 8.4% in 6 weeks by rebuilding their ICP filter."
Bad: "We deliver exceptional results for our clients through our proprietary methodology."

**Direct and concrete**
Say what you mean. Use numbers. Cut adjectives.

Good: "Reply rate was 2%. We rebuilt the targeting. It's now 8%."
Bad: "We dramatically improved their outbound performance through strategic refinement of their targeting approach."

**Short is better**
If you can say it in 60 words, don't use 100. If you can say it in one sentence, don't use three.

Rule: cut the first sentence of every draft. It's almost always warm-up.

---

## What we never say

These words and phrases are banned in all outbound copy:

- "synergy" / "synergize"
- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "holistic"
- "game-changing" / "game changer"
- "excited to share" / "I'm excited"
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "reach out" (say "contact" or "email")
- "circle back"
- "touch base"
- "move the needle"
- "unlock [potential/growth]"
- "best-in-class"
- "thought leader"
- "value-add"
- "low-hanging fruit"
- "boil the ocean"
- Any opener starting with "I" as the first word
- Any opener that compliments the company unprompted ("Love what you're doing at...")

## Subject line rules

- 2–4 words
- Lowercase only
- No punctuation
- Must feel like an internal forward, not a marketing email

Good: "quick question", "re: demand gen", "growth question", "referral from [name]"
Bad: "Boost Your Marketing ROI with [Agency Name]", "Partnership Opportunity — Let's Chat!"

---

## Structure rules by persona

**CMO (80 words max):**
Line 1: Specific observation about their company or a result from a comparable company
Line 2: Why it's relevant to them right now
Line 3: One-sentence offer
Line 4: Soft CTA ("Worth 20 minutes?")

**VP Marketing (100 words max):**
Line 1: Pain point observation or specific result
Line 2: Brief context — what you did and for whom
Line 3: Connection to their situation
Line 4: CTA ("Worth a 30-minute call?")

**Founder (60 words max):**
Line 1: The result, not the setup
Line 2: One sentence on how
Line 3: CTA ("15 minutes?" or a direct question)

---

## Angle rotation — follow-up rules

Each follow-up must use a DIFFERENT angle from the previous touch. Never repeat the same hook twice.

| Touch | Example angle |
|-------|--------------|
| Touch 1 | Case study or signal observation |
| Touch 2 | Different proof point or a question |
| Touch 3 | Short re-engage or breakup |
| Touch 4 | Close the loop — short and clean |

---

## Channel-specific rules

**Email:**
- Max lengths above apply
- One CTA per email
- One question max per email
- No links in first email unless essential

**LinkedIn (via Crispy):**
- Max 50 words
- No links in first message
- More conversational than email — shorter, warmer
- Connection note: 1 sentence + reason why

## What good looks like

First touch example (VP Marketing, SaaS):

Subject: demand gen question

"Noticed [Company] is hiring a demand gen manager — that usually means the team is maxed out on outbound execution.

We've run demand gen for three B2B SaaS companies at your stage. Last one went from 40 SQLs/month to 120 in 90 days.

Worth a 30-minute call to see if there's a fit?"
